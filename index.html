Delicious Chicken Biryani

A delightful and easy-to-make recipe for everyone to enjoy!

Ingredients

  • 500g Basmati Rice, washed and soaked
  • 1 kg Chicken , cut into pieces
  • 3 Large Onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 Tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup Plain Yogurt
  • 2 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • Green Chilies (to taste), slit
  • Fresh Mint Leaves, a handful
  • Fresh Coriander Leaves, a handful, chopped
  • Whole Spices: Bay Leaf, Cinnamon Stick, Cardamom Pods, Cloves
  • Powdered Spices: Turmeric Powder, Red Chili Powder, Biryani Masala
  • Oil or Ghee
  • Salt to taste
  • Saffron strands soaked in warm milk (optional)
  • Fried Onions for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, marinate chicken with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, red chili, biryani masala, salt, and half of the mint and coriander leaves. Let it sit for at least 30 minutes (or longer, refrigerated).
  2. Heat oil/ghee in a heavy-bottomed pot. Add whole spices and saut√© until fragrant.
  3. Add sliced onions and fry until golden brown.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and green chilies, cook until tomatoes soften.
  5. Add the marinated chicken and cook on medium-high heat for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until chicken is half-cooked and releases oil.
  6. Meanwhile, boil water in another pot. Add salt and a few drops of oil. Add the soaked basmati rice and cook until 70% done (al dente). Drain the rice immediately.
  7. Layering: In the pot with the chicken, spread half of the partially cooked rice evenly over the chicken.
  8. Sprinkle remaining mint and coriander leaves, and half of the saffron milk (if using) over the rice.
  9. Add the remaining rice layer. Sprinkle remaining saffron milk and fried onions (if using) on top.
  10. Cover the pot tightly with a lid (you can use dough to seal the edges for dum cooking).
  11. Cook on low heat for 15-20 minutes (dum cooking) until rice is fully cooked and flavors are absorbed.
  12. Gently mix the biryani before serving. Serve hot with raita.
